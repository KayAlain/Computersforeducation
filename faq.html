FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

CFSNB is a non-profit organization that refurbishes donated computers and distributes them to schools, non-profit organizations, libraries, and Indigenous communities across New Brunswick. Our goal is to provide access to technology and bridge the digital divide.

Eligible recipients include K-12 schools, post-secondary institutions, non-profit organizations, libraries, and Indigenous communities within New Brunswick.

To apply for computers, please visit our website and fill out the application form available under the "Apply" section. Provide all the required information, and we will get in touch with you to discuss your needs.

We accept donations of desktop computers, laptops, tablets, monitors, keyboards, mice, and other computer accessories. The equipment should be in working condition or repairable.

You can donate equipment by contacting us through our website or calling our office. We will provide you with instructions on how to drop off your donation or arrange for a pickup if needed.

Yes, donations to CFSNB are tax-deductible. We will provide you with a receipt for your donation, which you can use for tax purposes.

Donated equipment is refurbished by our team of technicians and volunteers. Once refurbished, the computers are distributed to eligible schools, non-profit organizations, libraries, and Indigenous communities to support their technology needs.

At this time, we only provide computers to eligible organizations and communities. However, individuals in need of technology can contact local non-profits that may have received computers from us and inquire about their programs.

We welcome volunteers to help with refurbishing computers, administrative tasks, and other activities. To volunteer, please fill out the volunteer application form on our website or contact our office for more information.

In addition to donating equipment, you can support CFSNB by making a monetary donation, volunteering your time, spreading the word about our program, or partnering with us on community initiatives.

Since its inception, CFSNB has distributed thousands of refurbished computers to schools, non-profits, libraries, and Indigenous communities across New Brunswick, enhancing access to technology and educational opportunities for many.

For more information, please visit our website or contact our office. We are happy to answer any additional questions you may have.
